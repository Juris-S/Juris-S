Implementing the AI for this project was a fun challenge. Usually, I'd use a NavMeshAgent to create my AI behaviours, and set a target destination using SetDestination(Vector3 target)

But, because I wanted to implement dynamic water physics, with rigidbodies and waves and a physics-based boat controller with drag, I figured a pathing mesh would be difficult to create for a dynamic water body.

The solution was to set the directions using dot product for movement, and a cross product to apply a directional force (left/right) toward the target object

Awards

Edge Hill University's (EHU) Excellence Scholarships reward those who demonstrate commitment, determination and achievement beyond the scope of their studies. I was fortunate to receive the award for my works spanning the technical field of Virtual Reality (VR) development and Theory of Mind (ToM) research in Psychology as well as professional collaborations with the Tohoku and Central European Universities. Alongside the research, I represented EHU at the British Psychological Society (BPS) annual Cyberpsychology Conference and presented several WiP projects concerned with gendered violence in video games and theory of mind in virtual reality Outside my academic research I elected to become a Psychology Student Representative and work as a Support Worker, providing care to those with disabilities

The Student Opportunity Fund provided by Edge Hill University (EHU) helps individuals take part in endeavours that enhance their employability, build confidence, and develop transferable skills. I was fortunate to receive the Student Opportunity Fund Award to fund me presenting my academic works at the British Psychological Society (BPS) annual Cyberpsychology Conference. I was one of the few undergraduates students who had an in-press publication and presented several in-progress papers to the academic and highly talented audience. I would not have had this opportunity if it wasn’t for the help of the educational team at EHU and my supervisors, Dr Gray Atherton and Dr Liam Cross, who always went the extra mile to promote my academic achievements and provide me with opportunities to broaden my abilities and knowledge, for which I am truly grateful.

Alongside the support of Dr Gray Atherton and Dr Liam Cross, I was successful in receiving the ISR Pump Prime grant from Arts and Humanities Research Council (AHRC) to fund my first-year undergraduate projects exploring how virtual realities can become safe and enriching spaces for those with disabilities to learn, explore and develop competencies. The spaces were co-designed with stakeholders to understand what type of virtual environments are most appealing to individuals with specific autism and intellectual disability.
